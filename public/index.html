Chat about what is happening in the world.

You open Hunchat.

As you scroll through the feed you see Larry’s video.

Why would you bother to use a new app?

The best communication happens face-to-face. But in this day and age, we have friends from all around the world and chatting with all of them is not easy due to time differences.

Enter asynchronous video conversations.

You can now chat with anyone, anytime, meet new people from all over the world through interesting conversations and learn by seeing what others are talking about.

The right ingredients

It takes a lot to build a better space for online conversations. These are some of the ingredients we used.

Video first approach

The ideal conversation happens face-to-face.
What is the closest we can get to that on a digital medium? Video.
‍
Asynchronous video allows you to speak with others even while you sleep.

Content separation

You don't talk about the same things with everyone, do you? Then why should you do it online?

Separate your online conversations by topics, just like you do in real life.

Thread structure

If you have used Twitter, you know what a thread structure is.

Participate in the conversations of many people and let others share their take on your thoughts.

Shared revenue

Creators should be rewarded by the interesting conversations they foster online.

A fare share of Hunchat's revenue is going directly to the pockets of the people who have conversations on the platform.

No ads

When was the last time an ad enhanced your online experience?

Advertising does not fit in a space made for conversations.

Open-source code

Privacy is important. We want to be as transparent with our final product as we are about building it.


Our code base can be accessed at https://github.com/hunchat
